Gold/Silver Ratio

How many ounces of silver can you buy with one ounce of gold? Many traders and precious metals investors will use this ratio to determine which metal to invest in, given the current ratio. A higher Gold/Silver ratio indicates that silver is undervalued relative to gold.

Investor Sentiment & Leading Indicators for Gold & Silver

What is the current sentiment for gold & silver? The following charts will show metrics that attempt to predict the future price of gold and silver.

Real Interest Rates vs Gold

Weak interest rates favor a higher price of gold.

The graph shows a strong inverse relationship between the price of gold and real interest rates: when real interest rates rise, the price of gold tends to drop and vice versa. As with any analysis of this kind, it's important to be prudent: even if the relationship is statistically significant, there can be divergences (as in 2005), and it is more realiable over the medium and long term.

If you expect real interest rates to decrease, gold appears to be a good bet / hedge.

Gold vs US Dollar Index

A weak dollar favors a higher price of gold, and vice-versa.

The graph shows a strong inverse relationship between the price of gold and the US Dollar Index: when the US dollar depreciates against a basket of other currencies, the price of gold tends to increase and vice versa.

If you expect the US Dollar to depreciate in relation to other major currencies, it is likely the price of gold will increase (at least in USD).

Gold vs Expansion of Central Bank Credit Expansion

Central bank credit expansion tends to favor an increase in the price of gold.

The graph shows a relationship between the price of gold and the M2 money supply, as reported by the Federal Reserve: when the M2 money supply increases, the price of gold tends to increase.

Gold Volatility Index

Silver Volatility Index

The CBOE Gold/Silver Volatility Index is a measure of expected price fluctuations in gold and silver options over the next 30 days. It is calculated in real time by the Chicago Board Options Exchange (CBOE).

The key words in that description are expected and next 30 days. The predictive nature of the index makes it a measure of implied volatility, not one that is based off historical data or statistical analysis. The time period of the prediction also narrows the outlook to the near term.

Gold Commitment of Traders

Silver Commitment of Traders

Commodity traders have access to a special market report each week that provides a snapshot of the positions of large institutional traders and small speculators in each commodity futures category. This information is called the commitment of traders report or “COT report” and is provided by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission.

The COT report essentially shows the net long or short positions for each available futures contract. If traders are overwhelmingly long or increasing their long positions then we will have a bullish bias on that market. Similarly, if traders are short or increasing their short positions then we will have a bearish bias.
